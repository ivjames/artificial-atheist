Existentialism begins with a stark premise: existence precedes essence. We are not born with a fixed purpose handed down from above; we arrive first, and the meaning of our lives is something we make.

## Freedom and its weight

For existentialist thinkers, this freedom is total — and that is precisely what makes it heavy. If no external authority defines what we should be, then every choice is ours to own. Responsibility cannot be outsourced.

## Meaning without guarantees

The search for meaning, on this view, is not the discovery of something already there but an act of creation. We build significance through commitment, relationships, and work — none of it underwritten by cosmic guarantee, all of it real for being chosen.

## Living the question

Existentialism does not resolve the anxiety of a meaningless universe so much as teach us to live honestly within it — to act, to commit, and to take authorship of a life that has no script.
