Some of the most important discoveries of recent decades happened not within a single discipline but in the spaces between them. Interdisciplinary research is increasingly where progress is made.

## Why borders are productive

Each field carries its own assumptions, methods, and blind spots. When disciplines meet, those blind spots are exposed and new questions become askable. Molecular biology, cognitive science, and climate modelling all emerged from exactly this kind of crossing.

## The friction is the point

Interdisciplinary work is harder — different vocabularies, different standards of evidence, different cultures. But that friction is what forces clarity and dislodges assumptions that a single field would never think to question.

## Building for collaboration

Institutions that reward narrow specialization alone leave value on the table. The future of science depends on creating room for the conversations that happen at the edges.
